Campbell River needs to build up, not out. By bringing land south of Jubilee Parkway into the city and encouraging development there, we are taking on infrastructure liability - the roads and pipes - that we cannot afford to maintain.

  • We have already built far more infrastructure than we can afford, requiring enormous tax rises over the next 20 years just to rebuild our crumbling roads and bursting pipes.
  • We cannot throw more wood on the fire; we cannot throw good money after bad.
  • It is not the developer's fault. It is ours - we have forgotten how to build places that can sustain themselves, and made it illegal.
  • We have to make the city we already have financially sustainable, or we will face ever growing tax increases and eventually bankruptcy.
  • First we need to legalise the type of gradual densification we want to see, then teach developers big and small how to build it.
  • This is not a zero sum game - both developers and the city can profit.
  • But currently we are massively subsidising subdivisions, and this has to stop. We have to be realistic and stop building infrastructure that can never pay for itself.
